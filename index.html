Cultural Organizations of New York City

Data 70600: Intro to JavaScript | Fall 2023

Ian Williams


This is an interactive map of cultural organizations in New York City, sourced from NYC Open Data. The data set was provided by the NYC Department of Cultural Affairs (DCLA).

I study organizations and am interested in what the taxonomy of cultural organizations is. Looking at their website, there is a list of organizations that provide services, but no map or guide.

Of note, from the data, over 500 organizations did not have geocoordinates and thus were filtered out from the dataset displayed here. No websites for the organizations were included in the data.

This project renders a spatial representation of the organizations, layered over NYC neighborhood boundaries we used in a class exercise, offering a way to explore the distribution of cultural organizations across neighborhoods.

Of course, the neighborhood definitions are also different in some cases, as evidenced in the pop-up labels.

Since this final project is a minimum viable product, I can't go into the level of detail I would like to. But I may examine further over time.

Some things to add could include chloropleth density across boroughs, custom icons for each type of organization, and a drop-down menu that can sort between the different types or display by borough.
